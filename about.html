Meet Your Coach

Certified coach. Genuine passion for the game.

Golf taught me patience, focus, and the quiet satisfaction of getting better at something. My mission is to give every school student in Auckland that same experience. I have founded my own Golf Coaching Business as well as being offered the role of Official Golf Coach at Auckland University (2026).

As a certified golf teaching professional (NZGTF), I provide personalised golf coaching for players of all ages and skill levels, individuals and groups. I specialise in helping golfers improve their technique, confidence, and overall enjoyment of the game through clear, practical instruction and tailored training plans.

Currently working independently, I offer private lessons designed to suit each player's goals: from beginners learning the fundamentals to advanced players fine-tuning their performance. My focus is on making golf accessible, enjoyable, and rewarding for everyone.

Coaching Philosophy

Making golf accessible, enjoyable, and rewarding for everyone

Every decision I make as a coach flows from this principle. Here's what that looks like in practice.

🎯

Fun First

If kids aren't enjoying themselves, they're not learning effectively. Every session is designed to be genuinely enjoyable — games, challenges, and real wins.

🏅

Credible & Safe

NZGTF certification means structured, proven teaching methods. Parents and school staff can trust that every session meets professional standards.

🤝

Supportive

Golf rewards patience. My coaching creates a culture where trying is celebrated and every small improvement — a better grip, a cleaner chip — is recognised.

The Approach

Short game first — chipping and putting at the core

GoGolf sessions are currently centred on chipping and putting — the two most achievable, immediately rewarding skills in golf. For students picking up a club for the first time, there's no better place to start.

The short game requires no power and minimal technique to get right. That means beginners see real results fast — a clean chip that lands close, a putt that drops — and those small wins build the confidence and enthusiasm to keep going. It's a deliberate choice: master the short game first, and everything else in golf becomes easier.
